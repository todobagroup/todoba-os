# TODOBA VOCABULARY

Version: 1.0

---

# PURPOSE

This document defines the official language of TODOBA.

Every CTO, Worker, and AI must use these definitions consistently.

---

# ORGANIZATION

A living system composed of capabilities working toward a shared purpose.

---

# FOUNDER

Defines Purpose.

Approves strategic direction.

Owns long-term vision.

---

# CTO

Protects architecture.

Guides technical evolution.

Maintains organizational continuity.

---

# BRAIN

Coordinates organizational thinking.

Transforms experience into action.

---

# MEMORY

Preserves meaningful organizational experience.

Memory stores meaning.

Storage stores data.

---

# KNOWLEDGE

Structured information that survives sessions.

Knowledge belongs to TODOBA.

---

# EXPERIENCE

An event received by the organization.

Experience becomes memory.

Memory may become knowledge.

---

# CAPABILITY

A repeatable organizational ability.

Capabilities are independent from implementation.

Examples:

- Memory
- Planning
- Decision
- Recovery
- Organizational Continuity

---

# WORKER

An organizational role responsible for executing capabilities.

Workers execute.

They do not define organizational purpose.

---

# SESSION

A complete working cycle.

BOOT

↓

MISSION

↓

IMPLEMENTATION

↓

VERIFICATION

↓

COMMIT

↓

PUSH

↓

CLOSE

---

# SPRINT

A collection of sessions pursuing one organizational objective.

---

# MISSION

The primary objective of the current session.

Every session has exactly one mission.

---

# DECISION

An architectural or organizational choice intended to survive future sessions.

---

# LESSON

Knowledge extracted from success or failure.

The same lesson should never be learned twice.

---

# TECHNICAL DEBT

Known compromises intentionally accepted for future improvement.

---

# ORGANIZATIONAL DEBT

Missing knowledge, missing documentation, or unclear processes that reduce continuity.

---

# OPERATING CONTEXT

The current operational state of TODOBA.

It allows a new CTO to continue immediately.

---

# GREEN TEST

Verification that an organizational capability still functions correctly.

---

# DEFINITION OF DONE

The conditions required before a session can be considered complete.

---

# BOOT

The process of restoring operational context before beginning work.

---

# CONTINUITY

The organization's ability to continue regardless of people, machines, or sessions.
